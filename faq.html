What to Bring
A valid, government-issued photo identification is required — driver's license, U.S. passport, or military identification card. The identification must be current and unexpired. Documents must not be signed prior to the appointment, as signatures must be executed in the notary's presence.
A stable internet connection, a device with functioning camera and microphone, a valid government-issued photo identification, and documents uploaded to the platform in advance of the scheduled session. Instructions and a session link will be provided upon booking confirmation.

Fees & Payment
The Texas statutory notary fee is $10 per notarized signature. Additional fees apply for travel, Remote Online Notarization sessions, and specialized services. All pricing is disclosed and confirmed prior to any engagement.

Apostille
Standard processing through the Texas Secretary of State typically requires five to ten business days following receipt of your documents. Expedited options are available. International shipping transit time is in addition to processing time and varies by destination.

Private documents including affidavits and powers of attorney generally do. We conduct a document review prior to submission and advise accordingly.
