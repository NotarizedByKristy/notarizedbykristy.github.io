Apostille & Document Authentication

Texas Secretary of State apostille coordination managed start to finish — for international clients, academic institutions, embassies, and anyone requiring document recognition abroad.

What Is an Apostille?

Your Document, Internationally Recognized

An apostille is an authentication certificate issued by the Texas Secretary of State that verifies the origin and legitimacy of a public document for recognition in foreign countries party to the Hague Convention of 1961.

If you are relocating abroad, enrolling in a foreign academic institution, applying for dual citizenship, conducting international business, or submitting documentation to a foreign embassy or consulate — an apostille is typically required.

Texas Notary Kristy manages the complete apostille coordination process: document review, notarization where required, submission to the Texas Secretary of State, and return shipping — domestic or international.

The Process

Apostille Coordination

We manage the complete workflow so you are not required to navigate state offices, shipping logistics, or document requirements independently.

1
Document Review

We review your documents to confirm eligibility, identify any notarization requirements, and establish the process and timeline for your specific document type and destination country.

2
Notarization

Where required by the Texas Secretary of State prior to apostille, we conduct notarization via mobile appointment or Remote Online Notarization session before submission.

3
SOS Submission

Documents are submitted to the Texas Secretary of State for apostille certification. Time-sensitive submissions are handled via tracked courier service.

4
Return & Delivery

Apostilled documents are returned via your preferred method. International clients receive documents via DHL International with full tracking provided at each stage.
