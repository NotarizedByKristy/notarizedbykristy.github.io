What Is an Apostille?

Your Document,
Recognized Worldwide

An apostille is a certification issued by the Texas Secretary of State that authenticates the origin of a public document so it will be recognized in foreign countries that are members of the Hague Convention.

If you're moving abroad, applying for dual citizenship, submitting academic credentials to a foreign university, or conducting international business — you likely need an apostille.

Texas Notary Kristy handles the entire process: document review, notarization if required, SOS submission, and international return shipping. You don't have to figure out the state process alone.

How It Works

You submit your documents for review. We confirm eligibility, identify any notarization requirements, and outline the exact process and timeline for your specific document type and destination country.

Common Questions

Apostille
FAQ

How long does the apostille process take?
Standard processing through the Texas Secretary of State takes 5–10 business days after we receive your documents. Rush options are available. International shipping adds additional transit time depending on destination.
Does my document need to be notarized before apostille?
It depends on the document type. Vital records like birth and marriage certificates issued by the state do not need notarization first. Private documents like affidavits, powers of attorney, and diplomas typically do. We review this with you before submission.
Can you help if I'm outside of Texas or the United States?
Yes. If your document was issued in Texas, we can coordinate the apostille regardless of where you currently are. RON sessions are available for any notarization needed, and we ship internationally via DHL.
What countries accept a Texas apostille?
All Hague Convention member countries — currently 120+ countries — accept apostilles. If your destination country is not a Hague member, a different authentication process (embassy legalization) is required. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.
Do you handle apostilles for documents from other states?
We specialize in Texas apostilles through the Texas SOS. For documents issued in other states, we can provide guidance but the apostille must be obtained from the issuing state's authority.
